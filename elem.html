üìñ The Dictionary of Sweet Words

Evaporation When water gets hot and turns into a cloud (steam). This is how we get the water out of the sap!
Sap The "energy drink" of the tree. It is mostly water with a little bit of sugar.
Sugarbush A forest or group of maple trees used for making syrup.
Tap (Spile) The small metal or plastic tube we put into the tree to help the sap drip out.
Xylem The tiny "straws" inside a tree trunk that carry the sap up to the branches.
Finishing The very last part of boiling when the sap finally becomes thick, sticky syrup.
